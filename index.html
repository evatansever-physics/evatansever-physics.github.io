Research Areas

What we study

From fundamental statistical physics to applied nanomagnetism, our work spans theory, simulation and collaboration with experimentalists worldwide.

01

Dynamic Phase Transitions

Nonequilibrium dynamics of spin systems driven by time-dependent oscillating magnetic fields, and the resulting dynamic phase diagrams.

02

Critical Exponents & Universality

Critical behaviour, scaling and universality classes of disordered and randomly diluted magnetic systems.

03

Quantum Monte Carlo

Quantum spin ladders, random-bond Heisenberg antiferromagnets and other strongly interacting quantum spin systems.

04

2D Magnetic Materials

Electronic and magnetic properties of two-dimensional layered materials such as RuCl₃, RuX₃ and metal halides.

05

Core/Shell Nanoparticles

Magnetic hyperthermia and magnetocaloric properties of core/shell nanoparticles with varying size, shape and anisotropy.

06

Binary Alloys & Relaxation

Finite-temperature properties of binary alloy systems and relaxation processes near equilibrium in magnetic materials.
